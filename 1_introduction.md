## ğŸ”— What is Blockchain?

A **blockchain** is a **distributed, decentralized, and immutable digital ledger** that records transactions across multiple computers (nodes) in such a way that the recorded data **cannot be altered retroactively** without changing all subsequent blocks and gaining network consensus.

In simple terms:  
> Blockchain is a system where data is stored in **blocks**, linked together in a **chain**, and maintained by **many participants instead of a single authority**.

---

## ğŸ§± Core Components of Blockchain

### 1. Block
Each block typically contains:
- Transaction data  
- Timestamp  
- Hash of the current block  
- Hash of the previous block  

This linking of hashes is what forms the **chain**.

---

### 2. Distributed Ledger
Instead of one central database:
- Every participant (node) has a **copy of the entire ledger**
- All copies are synchronized across the network

---

### 3. Decentralization
- No single entity controls the blockchain
- Decisions are made using **consensus mechanisms**

---

### 4. Immutability
- Once data is added, it **cannot be modified or deleted**
- Any attempt to change data breaks the cryptographic links between blocks

---

### 5. Consensus Mechanism
Rules that nodes follow to agree on:
- Which transactions are valid
- Which block is added next

Common examples:
- Proof of Work (PoW)
- Proof of Stake (PoS)

---

## ğŸ” Why Blockchain is Trustworthy

Blockchain eliminates the need for trust by using:
- Cryptography  
- Mathematics  
- Game theory  
- Distributed systems  

Trust is placed in **code and consensus**, not in people or organizations.
